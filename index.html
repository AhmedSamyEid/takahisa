About Us

TakaHisa Japanese Restaurant is the perfect place to have the unforgettable combination of premium authentic sushi and the best wagyu beef in the world. Our restaurant brings you into a luxurious Japanese fine dining experience in Dubai.

MICHELIN

Selected in the 2023 Michelin Guide for Dubai and the only Japanese restaurant to be awarded two toques in the Gault & Millau UAE 2023 listings, TakaHisa is an elegant and bespoke Japanese dining experience inclusive of the chef's finest, often seasonal ingredients selected exclusively for each guest. Located in TakaHisa Japanese Restaurant, Banyan Tree Dubai, First Floor, Dubai, United Arab Emirates

Gault & Millau

We have great news. It's our pleasure to announce that TakaHisa is the only Japanese restaurant to be awarded two toques in the Gault & Millau UAE 2023 guide.

It's an honour to be recognised amongst such a prestigious group of restaurants, all offering such stunning and high quality dining concepts. Thanks to you and your help that we were able to achieve this award, so I would like to extend our heartfelt thanks to you.

Furthermore, we have newly opened a Japanese restaurant called "ROBATA" on the 1st of June 2023.At Robata, we offer original omakase dining using the finest Japanese ingredients based on traditional robatayaki cooking. Please come and dine with us, we look forward to seeing you.

Our Chef

Chef Takashi Namekata

THE SUSHI MASTER

Chef Takashi was a member of Tokyo Ukai Group that had been chosen for Michelin Guide Tokyo for 7 consecutive years. Further developing and enhancing his traditional Japanese culinary skills, in 2015 he joined "TOMO" as an Executive Chef in Dubai. As a Sushi Master, he carefully selects fish of the finest quality in Toyosu Market and offers authentic Sushi.

THE WAGYU MASTER

Chef Hisao Ueda

Chef Hisao was born and grew up in Hokkaido, Japan, a place famous for its rich seafood, produce, and meat. His career started at a Japanese Kaiseki restaurant in Hokkaido where he honed his technical skills for about seven years before moving to Dubai in 2011. After serving as a Head Chef at some of the award-winning authentic Japanese restaurants, he opened Kohantei Japanese Wagyu beef restaurant in 2017 as an Executive Chef.
